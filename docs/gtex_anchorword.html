Goal:¶

I want to see if my fitted topics have anchor words (those genes that have proportions for some topics, and very small proportions in other topics) for all its topics.

Result:¶

Comment:¶

  • The fitted topic does not quite follow the anchor word assumption.
    • Why? For a few topics, the most dominant genes are also dominant in other topics

SEE In [6]

  • In one topic, top 10 genes take huge proportions (over 80%)

  • In most topic, top 10 genes take more than 10% proportions

n_top = 10
topics = {}

for i,x in enumerate(A.T):
    idx = np.argsort(x,)
    top_idx = idx[-n_top:]
    top_idx = top_idx[::-1]
    prop_top = x[top_idx].sum()
    top_genes = genes[top_idx]
    topics[i+1] = {"prop_top": prop_top, "top_genes":top_genes, "proportions":x[top_idx]}

prop_tops_ix = np.argsort([v["prop_top"]  for k,v in topics.items()])
prop_tops_ix = prop_tops_ix[::-1]
for i in prop_tops_ix:
    print("###-----------------------------###\n")
    print("TOPIC {} TOP GENES:\n".format(i+1))
    print("proportion of top {} genes: {}".format(n_top, topics[i+1]["prop_top"]))
    print("gene names\n")
    print(topics[i+1]["top_genes"])
    print("gene proportions")
    print(topics[i+1]["proportions"])
    print("\n")
